## STAY SAFE ONLLINE : Digital saftey checklist

Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. A PIN is only required to send money or check your balance. If someone asks for a PIN to "verify" a payment to you, it is a scam.

Secure your SIM card. Set a SIM lock/PIN in your phone settings to prevent scammers from using your SIM in another device to intercept OTPs if your phone is lost or stolen.

Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA). Use 2FA on WhatsApp, Instagram, and Gmail. This ensures that even if someone gets your password, they can't access your account without a second verification code.

Vet your study resources. Avoid downloading "cracked" software or unofficial PDFs from suspicious sites. These often contain keyloggers that can record your keystrokes and steal your login credentials.

Lock your social profiles. Use "Private" or "Friends Only" settings on platforms like Instagram and Facebook to prevent your photos from being misused for deepfakes or fake profiles.

Avoid banking on public Wi-Fi. Do not log into your bank or student portal while connected to campus cafes or public hotspots. If necessary, use a reputable VPN to encrypt your data.

Use only RBI-verified loan apps. If you need quick credit, check the RBI's list of verified lenders. Avoid "Instant Loan" apps that demand access to your entire contact list and gallery, as these are often used for extortion.

Verify customer care numbers. Never trust numbers found in Google Maps or image search results (e.g., for Zomato or Swiggy). Always use the official "Contact Us" page within the verified application.
